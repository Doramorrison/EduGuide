English

What is EFL in education?

EFL stands for (English as a First Language).First Language is a popular English Language qualification offered to pupils around the world. It is intended for pupils whose first language is English. It assesses pupils on their ability to communicate clearly, accurately and with style, and to read critically and with insight.


What is ESL in education?

ESL stands for (English as a Second Language) and refers to non-native speakers of English in countries where English might be less common. It means people who learn English as a secondary language to their own. Students all over the world study English as a Second Language, from Japan to Spain. Teachers from English-speaking countries such as the UK, the USA and Australia often travel abroad to teach English. Countries such as Vietnam, Spain, China, South Korea, Japan, and the UAE are popular locations for ESL teachers.


Which one is harder?

EFL is for students who speak English fluently and want to develop advanced reading, writing, and critical thinking skills. The exams focus on analyzing texts, summarizing, and writing creatively or persuasively, which requires a strong vocabulary and understanding of complex grammar.

ESL is designed for students who are still learning English as a second language and focuses on practical skills like listening, speaking, reading, and writing. The assessments are simpler, with tasks like writing emails or understanding basic texts.


Overall, EFL is harder than ESL because it focuses on advanced English skills, while ESL is for students who are still building their basic English.

Mathematics

Mathematics Extended

Extended mathematics is a program that builds on a standard math framework by adding more topics and skills to provide greater depth and breadth. It's designed for students who are more academically able and want to pursue higher education or professional training.


Mathematics Additional

Additional Mathematics encourages learners to further develop their mathematical ability in problem solving and provides strong progression for advanced study of mathematics or highly numerate subjects.


Mathematics Course Options

For university courses like Engineering, Computer Science, Economics, Medicine, or Physics, you'll need to take Mathematics A.

For university courses like Social Sciences, Humanities, or Arts, Mathematics E is enough.


Which One is Harder?

You can choose Mathematics (E) if you're not planning to use study subjects that requires advance math in the future.

Mathematics (A) if you're planning to study subjects that requires advance math, complex math solving skills in the future.This option will better prepared you for university and show that you're capable of handling very hard math questions.

Science

Biology

IGCSE Biology requires a diverse range of practical work to ensure students develop essential skills in scientific inquiry and experimentation. Practical activities include laboratory experiments, observations of biological processes, and analysis of results.

Chemistry

The IGCSE Chemistry syllabus focuses on key concepts such as atomic structure, chemical reactions, periodic trends, and bonding. Students gain hands-on experience through experiments that explore real-world chemical processes and principles.

Physics

IGCSE Physics is the study of matter, energy, motion, and forces. It examines the behavior of objects through space and time while addressing topics like mechanics, waves, electricity, and energy transfer. Practical experiments are integrated to strengthen understanding and problem-solving skills.

Combined Science

Combined Science gives students the opportunity to study Biology, Chemistry and Physics, each covered in separate syllabus sections. It is a single award qualification, earning one grade.

Business Studies

Business Studies is a field of study that deals with the principles of business, management, and economics. It combines elements of accountancy, finance, marketing, organizational studies, human resource management, and operations.


What it Focuses

It mainly focus on practical application of running a business and Management is also a major part of Business Studies, as it focuses on the skills and strategies involved in managing and operating businesses.

Economics

Economics helps us understand important topics like government policies like taxes and regulations, global trade, and the impact of things like technological changes or natural disasters on the economy. By studying Economics, we learn how choices are made about limited resources and how those choices affect everything from everyday life to the global economy.


How it works

It helps us understand why things cost what they do, how markets work, and how economic forces like supply and demand influence prices and choices.

Accounting

Accounting measures the results of an organization's economic activities and conveys this information to a variety of stakeholders, including investors, creditors, management, and regulators.


How it works

In accounting, you study how to record, classify, summarise, and interpret financial transactions. This includes learning about financial statements, taxation, auditing, and compliance with accounting standards and regulations.

ICT

Information and Communication Technology (ICT)

ICT is a broad subject, It covers any product that will store, retrieve, manipulate, process, transmit, or receive information electronically in a digital form (e.g., personal computers including smartphones, digital television, email, or robots).


What you can Learn

It prepares you for a career in Information and Communication Technology. It equips you with advanced skills in computer systems, programming, creating web pages, and basic animation.

Computer Science

The challenge of Computer Science lies in its rapidly changing nature, with new technologies and programming languages emerging regularly. Concepts such as algorithms, data structures, and cybersecurity can be intricate, demanding logical reasoning and problem-solving skills


What you can learn

Students learn to program by writing computer code and gain an understanding of the fundamental principles of problem-solving with computers. Using algorithms and a high-level programming language, students apply their knowledge to create computer-based solutions as well as understand the advantages of using them.

Geography

Geography, the study of the diverse environments, places, and spaces of Earth's surface and their interactions. It seeks to answer the questions of why things are as they are, where they are.


Why it's Important?

Studying geography helps us understand our environment, from helping us to predict natural disasters to implementing changes in response to issues such as global warming. From how weather systems work to natural phenomena, global population and migration, you will develop a strong knowledge of how the world works.

History

History is the study of change over time, and it covers all aspects of human society. Political, social, economic, scientific, technological, medical, cultural, intellectual, religious and military developments are all part of history.


Why It's Important?

Studying history helps us understand how events in the past made things the way they are today. With lessons from the past, we not only learn about ourselves and how we came to be, but also develop the ability to avoid mistakes and create better paths for our societies.

Sociology

Sociology is the study of society. You will learn about the society you live in and develop a wide range of knowledge and understanding about the world around you, and why things are the way they are!


What you can explore

Sociology is the study of social life, social change, and the social causes and consequences of human behavior. Sociologists investigate the structure of groups, organizations, and societies, and how people interact within these contexts.

Psychology

Psychology is the scientific study of mind and behavior. Its subject matter includes the behavior of humans and nonhumans, both conscious and unconscious phenomena, and mental processes such as thoughts, feelings, and motives.


Why choose Psychology?

Psychology can be an excellent choice for a college major and career for many reasons. Getting a psychology degree can open the doors to many different careers and lead you to a job that is in high demand.

Global Perspectives

IGCSE Global Perspectives encourages learners to think about and explore solutions to significant global issues.

What it teaches you

A global perspective helps students understand and appreciate the diverse cultures, viewpoints, and issues that shape our world. This understanding fosters empathy, adaptability, and critical thinking, which are essential skills in an interconnected global economy

Art and Design

Art and design must require students to know and. understand how sources inspire the development of ideas. For example, drawing on: the work and approaches of artists, craftspeople or designers from. contemporary and/or historical contexts, periods, societies and cultures.


What it teaches you

Art and Design is a challenging yet rewarding qualification for many students. It tests the student's knowledge, understanding, and application of creative processes in both two-dimensional and three-dimensional media.
